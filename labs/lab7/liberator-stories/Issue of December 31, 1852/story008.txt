 Six Years in a Georgia Prison. By Lewis W. Paine. This is the second edition of a narrative coveringabout two hundred pages, duodecimo, of a RhodeIsland man, who was thrown into prison in a saveState, because he exercised a common feeling of humanity. In other words, he helped a fugitive saveto escape from bondage. This was his crime. Hisadventures and sufferings, in and out of prisons, arebriefly and plainly told. The book is published byBela Marsh, Boston, and for sale by the author.
