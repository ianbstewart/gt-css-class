MAY'S SERMON ON THE CHARACTER OF DANIELWEBSTER This Sermon occupies our first pageand very worthily; being characterized by a deepmoral tone, an earnest spirit, and a reverent regardfor truth and justice, 'without respect of persons.For the privilege of laying it before our readers wewould proffer to its widely beloved author their thanksand ours. The awful demoralization of the Americanpulpit is demonstrated afresh in the fact, that, of hundredsof sermon, that have been delivered on thedeath of the great Northern Apostate, scarcely halfa doxen have spoken in any other terms than those ofthe most extravagant eulogy. Mr. May's is one ofthe honorable exception. R. AY'S ERMON ON THE HARACTER OF ANIEL EBSTER
